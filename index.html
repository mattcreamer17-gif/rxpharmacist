The frame

Most companies trying to add AI to an existing operation put language models where a database or a rule engine would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees. The honest split, in our experience, is 60% traditional code and database work, 30% rule-based logic, and 10% problems where a language model is actually the right tool.

For RxPharmacist specifically, the 10% is the tutor response layer and the statute-change summarization step. The other 90% is document parsing, diff detection, workflow routing, and pharmacist review queues. Getting that layering right is the difference between a tutor that ships in six weeks and a tutor that is still being debugged in nine months.

The case

We worked with a compliance-focused multi-agent team that had 160+ hours per filing of document review. The orchestration layer we designed (agent contracts, handoff boundaries, human-in-the-loop gates) collapsed that to under five minutes of machine time with traceable decisions a pharmacist-equivalent expert could sign off on.

The underlying problem is the same as annual MPJE refresh: a small expert team, a large regulated corpus, and a need to ship without burning anyone out.
